High-Value Target — Roush Enterprises
Strategic Opportunity

This month I made an initial connection with someone from Roush Enterprises. This is a relationship worth investing in seriously. Roush is one of the most capable and well-resourced contract engineering and manufacturing companies in Michigan, and if we can find the right way in and earn their trust, they have the potential to become one of Sharp-Eyed's largest U.S. clients.

Who they are: Roush Enterprises is a privately held engineering, manufacturing, and testing company headquartered in Livonia, Michigan. Founded in 1995, they serve automotive OEMs (Ford, GM, and others), motorsports teams, aerospace programs, and defense clients. Their core work includes vehicle prototyping and development, contract manufacturing, specialty vehicle assembly, and production testing. They run a significant in-house machining operation (90,000 sq. ft. facility) but also source precision components externally for high-volume and specialty programs.

Why this matters for Sharp-Eyed: Companies like Roush run complex, multi-material programs requiring precision-machined aluminum, steel, and hardened steel components at consistent quality. Their external sourcing needs, particularly for automotive and aerospace-grade fabricated parts, align directly with Sharp-Eyed's capabilities and certifications (IATF 16949, ISO 9001). The spend potential, estimated at $200M–$275M annually across fabricated and machined parts, makes this one of the highest-value targets in our pipeline.

Next steps:

  • Nurture the existing contact — focus on building personal trust before pushing any commercial conversation.
  • Research which programs or categories Roush currently sources externally and where Sharp-Eyed fits.
  • Prepare a targeted company introduction with relevant certifications and sample parts most relevant to their programs.
  • Patience is key here. Roush is a sophisticated buyer; a long, trust-first approach is the right one.

* Fabricated/precision parts spend is a modeled estimate based on industry benchmarks (40–55% of revenue for contract manufacturers). Roush is privately held and does not publish procurement figures.
