## 3. Taiwan-US Angle as Differentiator

This is your sharpest competitive edge, and you are underusing it.

### The Tariff Landscape (as of 2026)

- US imports from Taiwan have surged — Taiwan surpassed China as a US import source for the first time in decades
- Taiwan's hardware and precision manufacturing sector is seen as the most resilient partner amid US tariff hikes on global competitors
- Taiwan's tariff situation on machine tools and metal parts to the US has been volatile but generally more favorable than China's (32%+ on Chinese goods vs. fluctuating rates on Taiwanese goods)
- "Made in Taiwan" carries none of the stigma or supply chain risk of "Made in China" among US procurement managers

### Your Pitch: "Taiwan Quality + US Support"

The problem most US companies have with overseas manufacturing is distance — time zones, language barriers, quality concerns, and no one local to call when something goes wrong. You solve that:

**"Sharp-Eye manufactures in our state-of-the-art Taiwan facility — same quality standards as any US shop at 30-50% lower cost. But unlike other overseas suppliers, I live here. I'm two hours away. If there's an issue, you call me, not a time zone 12 hours ahead."**

### Practical Ways to Leverage This

1. **Lead with certifications, not geography.** IATF 16949 and ISO 13485 (if you pursue medical — see Section 4) matter more than where the parts are made. The Taiwan connection is the closer, not the opener.
2. **Get a US phone number that forwards.** You already have this. Make it prominent everywhere.
3. **Offer a "US Bridge" guarantee.** Parts are shipped to your US address for QC spot-checking before final delivery to the customer. This removes the fear of receiving a container of bad parts with no recourse.
4. **Reference Taiwan's IP protection record.** Taiwan has strong intellectual property protections compared to China. For customers with proprietary designs, this matters enormously.
5. **Check the US-Taiwan trade relationship quarterly.** Tariff rates and trade agreements shift. Taiwan hardware exporters are navigating a complex environment — stay informed through the Taiwan External Trade Development Council (TAITRA) and your industry associations.

### One-Liner for Cold Outreach

> "We manufacture precision parts in our IATF 16949-certified Taiwan facility, but I'm based in Indiana — you get Asian manufacturing economics with a local partner who answers the phone."

## 6. Partnership Strategies

You cannot be everywhere. Partnerships multiply your reach without multiplying your headcount.

### Manufacturers' Representatives

Independent sales agencies that sell complementary (non-competing) product lines into the same buyers you want. They work on commission (typically 5-10% of sales) and already have relationships with your target customers.

**How to find them:**
- **MANA RepFinder** (manaonline.org) — The largest directory of manufacturers' reps in the US. Search by territory (Midwest) and industry (machined parts, metal components).
- **Walk the D2P show floor** — Many reps have booths or walk these shows. Ask exhibitors of complementary products who reps them.
- **Ask your GTI contacts** — Daniel Erbacher at GTI likely knows reps who call on his industry.
- **IME West / MD&M exhibitor lists** — Look for rep agencies with booths; they're actively seeking new lines.

**Rep agreement structure:**
- Territory-exclusive for 12 months, renewable based on performance
- 5-8% commission on sales they generate
- You handle quoting and production; they handle relationships and closing
- Start with one rep agency covering MI/OH/IN, expand from there

### Complementary Manufacturing Partners

Find US-based assembly shops, plating shops, or larger contract manufacturers who might be great referral sources:

- **Assembly shops** that don't do machining — you become their machining partner
- **US machine shops** at capacity — you take their overflow
- **Plating/finishing shops** — since your affiliate A-KING does electroless nickel plating, you can offer a combined machining + plating package

**Where to find them:**
- PMA member directory
- ThomasNet supplier search in your target regions
- LinkedIn searches for "CNC Machine Shop" + city name

**The conversation starter:** _"We're not competing with you. We're giving you an offshore option you can offer your customers that still has a local contact managing the relationship."_

### Strategic Account Strategy for GTI

GTI Fabrication is currently your primary client. Protect and grow this relationship before chasing new ones.

- Ask Daniel Erbacher: "Do you have other suppliers at GTI who might need what we do?"

## 7. Government & Defense

### DLA (Defense Logistics Agency) Opportunities

SharpEye can pursue US Department of Defense contracts. The DLA actively seeks to expand its industrial base and has specific programs for small businesses.

**Steps to qualify:**
1. **Register in SAM.gov** — The System for Award Management is mandatory to bid on any federal contract. This is free but requires patience (2-4 weeks for processing).
2. **Get a CAGE code** — Assigned during SAM registration. Identifies your company to the federal government.
3. **Register with DLA Internet Bid Board System (DIBBS)** — Where DLA posts solicitations for manufactured parts.
4. **Look for "small business set-asides"** — Contracts reserved for small manufacturers. SharpEye likely qualifies.
5. **NAICS codes to target:** 332710 (Machine Shops), 332721 (Precision Turned Parts), 332116 (Metal Stamping), 332112 (Nonferrous Forging), 332510 (Hardware Manufacturing).

**What the DLA buys:** Replacement parts for military vehicles, aircraft components, weapon system parts, fasteners, bushings, actuators — many of these are the same types of parts you already make for GTI.

**Realistic timeline:** 6-12 months from SAM registration to first contract. The paperwork is significant but the contracts are long-term and reliable.

**Important note:** As a foreign-owned (Taiwanese) manufacturer, you'll need to verify compliance with DFARS (Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement) and any ITAR restrictions. Your US office in Delaware helps establish a domestic entity. Consult a government contracts attorney for the specifics — it's a few hundred dollars well spent.

### State Incentives

**Indiana:**
- Indiana Economic Development Corporation (IEDC) offers tax credits for job creation and capital investment
- The Manufacturing Readiness Grant program provides matching grants for technology adoption
- If SharpEye ever establishes a US-based facility, these become very relevant

**Ohio:**
- JobsOhio offers incentives for businesses creating jobs in Ohio
- Foreign Trade Zones (FTZs) in Ohio can reduce or defer tariffs on imported materials — potentially relevant for your Taiwan-to-US shipments
- Harrison, OH area may have local economic development incentives through Hamilton County

**Action:** Call the Dearborn County (IN) and Hamilton County (OH) economic development offices. Ask what programs exist for a company with a US-based country manager looking to grow domestic business. These conversations are free and can uncover unexpected support.
