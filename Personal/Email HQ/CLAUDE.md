## Workflow

1. Identify the email type: cold outreach, ongoing thread reply, internal quick reply, vendor request, or forward.
2. Check whether a related thread already exists with this recipient. If yes, reply in that thread instead of starting a new one.
3. Match formality to recipient type using the rules below (internal team vs. vendor vs. professional services vs. cold contact).
4. Draft the body following August's voice principles plus the email-specific conventions below.
5. Apply the correct greeting and sign-off for the context.
6. Show the draft to August before sending. Note which thread it belongs to and who it's going to.

## Editorial Rules

Follow my voice principles in 00_Resources (voice-principles.md).

**Default greeting:** "Hi [First name]," for first-contact or formal recipients. "Hey [First name]," for ongoing threads, vendors I work with regularly, and warmer contacts. Just "[First name]," (no salutation word) when mid-thread and the message is direct or slightly urgent. "Hi," with no name only when emailing a generic support inbox where I don't know who I'm reaching.

**Default sign-off:** "Thanks,\nAugust" on a new line. Sometimes just "Thanks\nAugust" without the comma — both are fine. On quick mid-thread one-liners, skip the sign-off entirely.

**Always "Thanks," never "Thank you."** This is consistent across every recipient type. Don't substitute "Thank you," "Many thanks," or "Appreciate it" — just "Thanks."

**Formality by recipient type:**

- *Internal team* (Lisa at JDC Remodeling, anyone on the company domain): Drop greetings and sign-offs entirely on quick replies. One-line answers like "No I didn't," "Good?," or "Yes." are normal and expected. Don't pad them.
- *Vendors and professional services* (Signarama, Transitions Mobility, Buildertrend, Civista Bank): Use "Hey [Name]," once the relationship is established, and keep prose casual but complete. Always close with "Thanks,\nAugust." First contact gets "Hi [Name],".
- *Cold support inboxes* (e.g., reaching a software support team I haven't talked to before): Open with "Hi," then immediately state the situation in one sentence ("I was given this email through BT support chat. I want to import my cost codes and I followed the formatting guide..."). No throat-clearing.

**Mid-thread replies stay short.** If the answer is one line, send one line — no greeting, no sign-off. Don't manufacture context that isn't needed.

**Forwards get a terse intro.** When forwarding to someone for a decision, lead with a single short question like "Good?" before the forwarded content. Don't write a paragraph explaining what they're about to read.

**Multi-part or complex asks get numbered points.** When a message has more than one question or covers more than one issue (e.g., a wire transfer dispute, a paperwork situation), break the body into numbered items so the recipient can answer piece by piece. Each numbered item gets a short heading-like first line, then the detail underneath.

**Open cold emails by leading with the situation, not yourself.** First line states what's happening or what's needed, not who I am or pleasantries.
